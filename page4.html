Are Filipino Americans Asian?

Watch this video to learn about how college students perceive Filipino Americans.

Many Filipino Americans identify as Pacific Islander, but what does that mean? Pacific Islanders are a diverse group of people from the islands of the Pacific Ocean. Three major subregions: Melanesia, Micronesia, and Polynesia. They are not all the same, but Philippines are not considered a Pacific Islander! Filipinos are actually considered Asian, which is a racial category to label those from south and east Asian countries, including the Philippines. But Asian is a broad word. You can either be Vietnamese, Japanese, or Korean.

Source: Penbrothers - Are Filipinos Pacific Islanders?

Are Filipino Americans real Asians?

Watch this video to learn how Filipino Americans are perceived

There are a lot of Filipino Americans in the United States today, and many feel like myself as the evidence shows. Being Filipino American does not make us less Filipino or American. We are both, a blend of both cultures. We may not be accepted by those born and raised in the Philippines. But to be a true Filipino, whether raised in the Philippines or in the United States, is to have a sense of togetherness, community, and love for each other. We can be more in touch with our roots in many different ways. One being mythology! You may have heard about Greek Mythology, but do you know about Filipino Mythology? Even if you aren't Filipino, you can learn more about the mythology of a different country and you can take the knowledge you learned and apply it your life.
