I feel ligalig for who I am. Behind proudness is uncertainty of identity. My parents were raised in the Philippines, immigrated to the United States. Myself, born and raised in the United States. A land with opposite culture and morals. A land of freedom but divided. Force fed to speak English and study American way of life. But I don't feel American. I don't feel Filipino either. I feel ligalig for my mother tongue being lost. Dances, traditions, and stories fading away with me. The culture of my ancestors in the islands meaning nothing now that being born here caused me to be foreign to my own origin. I feel ligalig that I feel out of place in the land I was born in. That I would trade my "ethnic" food for what everyone else had. That I wanted to be something else. To be laughed at for my skin, my eyes, my height.

With this I ask, am I a real Filipino? Am I a real American?
